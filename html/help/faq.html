How does Glossre work?

Glossre’s mobile web app enables you to upload or take photographs of a space and quickly organize them by area. Glossre allows you to then curate an interactive slideshow by adding in-photo tags, sharable and with friends via Glossre, Facebook, Twitter or just plain email. You can also print specific slides to a PDF brochure if you want a hard copy or prefer a non-interactive presentation of your space.

What is a Glossre Page™?

A Glossre Page™ is a space’s profile page — an interactive slideshow presentation of your home. Create and share your home page, literally, to unlock your home's value.

What makes a good Glossre Page™?

A good Glossre Page™ tells the story of a space to its audience; the more personal, the better. Start by taking good pictures and then be sure to add a lot of notes and annotations. With the ability to add in-photo author’s notes, compliments, comments and questions, there are limitless ways to share your story. Here are some tips for how to photograph a space and get off to a good start on Glossre:

General

  1. Turn on all lights.
  2. Close shades at least slightly.
  3. Try and shoot at a time when natural light is pouring in.
  4. Stay away from shooting at night for interiors.
  5. Dusk is a great time for shooting exteriors because it enhances details.
  6. Some rooms warrant directional light. 
  7. Link spaces together so that one photo peeks into the next.
  8. Be aware that the viewer’s eye want to go to the brightest spot in a photograph so try and keep it dark in the foreground and bright in the background.
  9. For speedy content, stand in one corner and shoot diagonally across into another corner using the widest lens possible.
  10. Emphasize architectural or design components for interesting shots.

Single Family Home

  1. Two photos of the front of your house, and one of each additional side.
  2. A sense of entry into your house, like your front door and foyer.
  3. Two photos of each living area.
  4. Photos of your kitchen should show how it connects to other areas.
  5. Objects of interest, like art and furniture.
  6. Points of interest, like a stairwell.
  7. Bathrooms should only be photographed in a way that is additive or relevant to your audience.

Apartment/Condo/Building

  1. A photo of the front of your building.
  2. A sense of entry into your house, like your front door and foyer.
  3. One photo of each living area.
  4. Photos of your kitchen should show how it connects to other areas.
  5. Objects of interest, like art and furniture.
  6. Points of interest, like a stairwell.
  7. Bathrooms should only be photographed if additive or relevant to your audience.
  8. Capture your view out to the world.

Is sharing my address with Glossre required?

No. Sharing your address is optional. Privacy is important to us, so please rest assured we will never compromise your privacy by revealing your address in the event you do choose to share it with us. You may, of course, choose to display your address publicly, by naming your space accordingly, particularly if you are using your Glossre Page™ to help sell your home.

What is Glossre’s approach to privacy?

Glossre believes in reclaiming the image of your home that probably already exists online. Homes have a digital footprint populated by the local tax assessor, previous listings or even the street view on a mapping app. If you and/or your home are going to be searched, the result should be the image you want to project of this very important facet of your life. At a minimum, the front door view of your house should be the view you want snoopers to see. By default, your information can only be seen by your friends, or Homeys™ as we call them.
